Everything you need to know before and during your trip

üéå Japanese Etiquette ‚Äî Dos & Don'ts

‚úì DO

Bow when greeting ‚Äî A slight nod or bow shows respect. The deeper the bow, the more formal.

‚úó DON'T

Tip at restaurants ‚Äî Tipping is considered rude in Japan. The service is already excellent and included.

‚úì DO

Remove shoes indoors ‚Äî When entering homes, temples, ryokans, and some restaurants, always remove your shoes.

‚úó DON'T

Eat or drink while walking ‚Äî It's considered impolite. Find a bench or stand by the food stall to eat.

‚úì DO

Be quiet on trains ‚Äî Keep phone calls and conversations to a whisper. Set your phone to silent (manner mode).

‚úó DON'T

Stick chopsticks upright in rice ‚Äî This resembles a funeral ritual and is considered very disrespectful.

‚úì DO

Carry your trash ‚Äî Public trash cans are rare. Bring a small bag to carry garbage until you find one.

‚úó DON'T

Block escalators ‚Äî Stand on the left side in Tokyo (right in Osaka). Keep one side open for those walking.

‚úì DO

Say "itadakimasu" before eating ‚Äî It means "I humbly receive" and shows gratitude for the meal. Say "gochisousama" after.

‚úó DON'T

Blow your nose in public ‚Äî It's considered rude. Step away to a restroom if you need to. Sniffling is more acceptable.

‚úì DO

Shower before entering an onsen ‚Äî Always wash thoroughly at the shower stations before getting into the communal bath.

‚úó DON'T

Touch the deer aggressively in Nara ‚Äî They're sacred but can bite/headbutt! Be gentle and bow to them ‚Äî they may bow back.
